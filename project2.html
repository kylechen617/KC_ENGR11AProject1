Project 2

We were tasked with remixing an existing 3D model that we found online from thangs or 3D nih using Tinkercad and Fusion. I chose to remix a apple wireless charger stand for my phone.

Tinkercad Remix

When I downloaded the model it originally came with an extra holder for an apple watch charger like the image to the right. My final product in the image above came after remixing a few things. Because I don't own an apple watch I decided to omit the feature by using a hole feature in tinkercad along with adding a few small extrudes to cover up where the cut out for the apple watch wire went on the back of the stand. The bottom did create a bit of a problem for me because it resulted in a rectangular prism jutting out from the bottom of the curved base so I had to use the scribble too and make a very janky fix by scribbling my own curved hole feature to create a new curve to the base.

Fusion Remix

Uploading my .stl file was a very difficult task at the start for this part of my project. Even after following the steps of the tutorial I was unable to turn the mesh into a body that could be modified. Most of the problem came down to an error telling me that I had over 10,000 trianlges in my mesh.

The Print

After finally going to after hours I was made aware of that Fusion is very bad at handling stl files. With a little more help I was able to import the file and remove the watch stand once again. However, the poor implementation of the 10,000 triangles created a very difficult user experience when trying to fillet the edges together as well as fill the holes left by the wire cut out. Whenever I would attempt to fillet an edge, it would end up giving me errors because of the multiple flat surfaces created from the unnecessary triangles. Creating sketches on the faces of the stand also had a problem because there were often too many edges that were slightly off. It was what ended up creating the little bit of filament fuzz on the first layer of my stand. As a result, I ended up running out of time and printed the basic model from above.
